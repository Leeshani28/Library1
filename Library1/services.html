-Services-

Borrow & Reserve items

Find out how to borrow, return, order and reserve items from the Lancaster Libraries.


-Borrow items

Who can borrow?

Current members can borrow items from our lending libraries. Registered to our Trust readers with a valid Lancaster Libraries card can borrow up to 10 items at any one time from our Libraries. Lancaster Reader card holders can use items while in our libraries, but they cannot borrow.


How to borrow

If you're eligible to borrow items, you can use a self-scan machine (if available) or check out at the library information desk. If you're unable to visit a library in person to borrow items, please nominate someone to collect available books on your behalf by emailing full details to the relevant library.


-Reserve items on loan to someone else

If all of the copies of a book that you need to use are out on loan, you can place a reservation. This will prevent further renewals. You will be contacted by email when the book is ready for you to borrow.


Print,Copy & Scan

You can use our Print, Copy and Scan (PCAS) facilities to print documents and to copy library items. Readers are welcome to use personal digital cameras and scanners, subject to restrictions.
The library provides a photocopy service at subsidized rates through a private service provider.Users are requested to use this facility to copy only the library materials.


Link your library card

You can link your Reader card to your PCAS account on any PCAS-enabled library device. Tap your card on the touchpad and enter your Lancaster Libraries username and password when prompted. Next time, you can tap your card on the machine to log in.


Inter Library loan

Inter Library Loan is a service that will assist users in obtaining research materials that are not available in the library though available in other libraries. Books and photocopies of journal articles can be requested from other libraries if available there. Users who wish to avail themselves of this facility should apply on the ILL form (MS word, PDF) available in the web or prescribed form available in the circulation counter. Material obtained in this way may be used in the library.


Reading Room facilities


The library has three reading rooms:
Periodical Room (Ground Floor)
Reading Room I (Ground Floor)
Reading Room II (First Floor)

Periodical Room
Current issues of periodicals in social science and humanities are kept in this reading room. Back issues of periodicals in the current year are also kept in the same shelves along with the current one. 55 seats are available in the room for use by the library users.

Reading Room I
Permanent and quick reference collections such as bibliographies, encyclopedias, dictionaries, year books, directories and other reference works are kept in this reading room. This room accomodates 154 users.


Reading Room II
This is the main undergraduate reading room where scheduled reference as well as permanent reference materials are kept. There are 326 seats available for use by the library users.

Help Desk


The help services on this page are for:
lancaster Reader card holders
prospective researchers needing information specifically about or held by the lancaster Libraries.

We are happy to help with any questions about using the lancaster Libraries for your academic study or research, from understanding your reading list and finding items to helping you with passwords and access to ebooks and journals. You can chat to a librarian online, call us, send us an email, browse the training and workshops we offer, or explore our guides to using our services.
